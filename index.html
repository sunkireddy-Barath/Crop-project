Types of Crops

Rice

Staple crop grown in flooded fields; requires high water and warm climates.

Wheat

Versatile cereal crop grown in temperate regions; used for bread, pasta, and more.

Maize (Corn)

Highly productive crop used for food, feed, and industrial products.

Soil Types and Their Benefits

Loamy Soil

Rich in nutrients, excellent water retention and drainage — best for most crops.

Sandy Soil

Drains quickly, warms fast — ideal for crops like carrots, potatoes, and peanuts.

Clay Soil

High in nutrients, retains moisture — good for rice and broccoli, but requires proper drainage.

Silt Soil

Fertile and smooth — retains moisture well and supports lush plant growth.
